Lee Scratch Perry

Real Name Rainford Hugh Perry

Pioneer of Dub and Roots Reggae in Jamaica

"Without music you are all dead; with music you are alive. There is nothing music can't do. "

by "Lee Scratch Perry"

  • 1936 - Born March 28,Kendal, Jamaica
  • 1959 - As a dancer and as a labourer in Negril and Westmoreland, went to Kingston with the aim of working in the music industry
  • 1967 - Perry worked with producers like Prince Buster and Clancy Eccles and started a cooperation with Joe Gibbs.
  • 1968 - He founded the label Upsetter Records. In the studio with Lynford Anderson ("Andy Capp"), his cousin Barry Lambert and an intern - the Upsetters.
  • 1972 - After the collaboration with Bob Marley. Bob Marley remained in contact with Perry. Later, Marley recorded songs from his time for example "Kaya" & "Sun Is Shining" in 1978.
  • 1973 - He built his famous Black Ark Studio
  • 2011 - He released the album "Rise Again", on which he was able to gather many music greats.


    • Privates - In the 1970s, Perry lived with his then wife Pauline "Isha" Morrison and their three children in Kingston's Washington Gardens area of Cardiff Crescent. In 1989, he married Mireille Campbell in Switzerland in a Krishna ceremony. The couple have two children. They now live in Einsiedeln, Switzerland. He is known in the village as a "crazy bird of paradise" who likes to decorate his outlandish clothes with CDs.



  • 1998 - The 1975 album "Revolution Dub" was included in The Wire's legendary "100 Records That Set the World on Fire (While No One Was Listening)" list.
  • 2003 - He won the Grammy in the category Best Reggae Album with his album "Jamaican E. T.".
  • 2013 - He received the Musgrave Medal of the Institute of Jamaica for his services.


  • 2021 - He past away in August 29 in Lucea, Jamaica
